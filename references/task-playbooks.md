# Task Playbooks for SEO/GEO

Use this reference for complete work. Do not deliver generic checklists. Decide what matters based on the website, intent, risk and available evidence.

## 1. Complete SEO/GEO Audit

1. Define scope: domain, subdomains, country, language, CMS, goal, period, competitors and available sources.
2. Collect signals:
   - Sitemap, robots, status codes, canonicals, meta robots and rendered HTML.
   - Existing crawl or own crawl when viable.
   - GSC: clicks, impressions, CTR, position, indexation, sitemaps, CWV and manual actions.
   - Bing Webmaster Tools: search performance, indexation and AI Performance when available.
   - Analytics: organic traffic, conversions, revenue or leads and landing pages.
   - Ahrefs, Semrush or Sistrix: keywords, backlinks, top pages and competitors.
   - SERPs: intent, formats, features and real competitors.
   - Logs: bots, crawl waste, status codes and frequency.
3. Diagnose by layers:
   - Technical: crawl, index, render, canonical, redirects, architecture, performance, mobile, schema and hreflang.
   - Content: intent, quality, coverage, E-E-A-T, differentiation, freshness, thin content, duplication and cannibalization.
   - Authority: links, mentions, internal links, topical authority and reputation.
   - GEO: citable content, entity clarity, visible HTML, sources, consistency and AI citations.
4. Prioritize P0/P1/P2/P3 with impact, effort, risk, evidence and owner.
5. Deliver an action plan, not an infinite inventory.

P0 usually includes accidental noindex, broken robots, severe canonical errors, failed migrations, major traffic drops, critical duplication, money pages blocked from crawling and 5xx errors.

## 2. Technical Audit

Review:

- robots.txt: blocks, sitemap declaration, rules by user agent and AI bots when relevant.
- Sitemaps: indexable URLs, 200 status, self-canonicals, honest lastmod and exclusion of noindex, redirects and 404s.
- Status: 200, 3xx, 4xx, 5xx and soft 404s.
- Indexability: meta robots, X-Robots-Tag, accidental noindex and wrong canonical.
- Canonicals: duplicates, parameters, variants, pagination and international versions.
- Redirects: chains, loops, incorrect 302s and lost mapping.
- JS/rendering: initial HTML, final DOM, links in DOM, critical content and hydration errors.
- Architecture: depth, hubs, breadcrumbs, orphan pages and links to money pages.
- Facets/parameters: index, noindex, canonical or block depending on demand and value.
- Performance: LCP, INP, CLS, images, JS, fonts, cache and third parties.
- Mobile: layout, tap targets, equivalent content and intrusive interstitials.
- Schema: validity, visible content match, duplication and correct types.
- Hreflang: codes, reciprocity, compatible canonicals and x-default.
- Security: HTTPS, mixed content, malware and hacked pages.

Minimum output: issue, evidence, affected URLs, impact, technical action and validation.

## 3. Keyword Research and Intent

Do not start with volume. Start with business and intent.

1. Define offer, ICP, market, funnel and conversions.
2. Build universes:
   - Problem or pain.
   - Solution or category.
   - Product or service.
   - Comparisons.
   - Alternatives.
   - Competitors.
   - Integrations.
   - Local.
   - Expert informational.
   - Objections and questions.
3. Classify intent: informational, commercial, transactional, navigational, local or support.
4. Validate SERP: page types, features, depth, freshness, brand/directories/marketplace dominance and whether a new page or existing page improvement is needed.
5. Assign one primary intent per URL. Secondary intents can support it, but avoid near-duplicate pages.
6. Prioritize by business potential, offer fit, real competition, current gap, authority needed and effort.

Terms such as "ownership" and "cannibalization" are internal language. Do not publish them on final pages.

## 4. SEO/GEO Brief

A brief must let a writer, developer or client implement without guessing.

Include:

- Proposed URL and canonical.
- Page type.
- Primary intent and secondary intents.
- User, funnel, offer and CTA.
- Main keyword/entity and variants.
- Entities and questions.
- SERP observations and competitors.
- Differentiated angle.
- Required proof.
- H1/H2/H3 structure.
- GEO blocks.
- Internal links.
- External links.
- Schema.
- Media.
- Risks.
- Metrics.
- QA requirements.

## 5. Existing URL Optimization

1. Identify the real goal of the URL.
2. Review current performance when available: GSC, analytics, conversions and rankings.
3. Review current SERP and intent.
4. Detect mismatch in title, H1, intro, proof, entities, thin content, duplication, CTA, links or schema.
5. Propose before/after for title, description, H1, intro, H2/H3, FAQs, GEO blocks, links and schema.
6. Validate intent, cannibalization, quality and absence of internal language.

## 6. Content Creation

Process:

1. Confirm intent and audience.
2. Create the brief.
3. Design structure.
4. Answer the main intent quickly.
5. Develop real depth.
6. Add proof, examples, data or experience.
7. Add nuance and limitations.
8. Add tables/lists only when they improve understanding.
9. Add real FAQs.
10. Add title, description, internal links, schema and CTA.
11. Review for humans, search engines and AI systems.

Do not write generic text that could belong to any domain. Every piece needs an angle, business context, concrete examples or proof.

## 7. Architecture and Topical Authority

Build maps with pillar pages, support pages, transactional pages, informational pages, comparison pages, local pages, entity pages, hubs, breadcrumbs, vertical/horizontal links, anchors, priority and cannibalization risk.

Rules:

- Do not create pages if there is no differentiated intent or value.
- Do not split by keyword when users expect one answer.
- Consolidate when two URLs compete for the same intent.
- Create a new page when SERP, user, offer or expected format is different.
- Link from pages with authority and context.

## 8. Competitive Analysis

Analyze real SERP competitors, not only business competitors.

Review intent, page type, depth, structure, proof, authorship, trust, internal links, schema, UX, performance, entities, snippets, backlinks, freshness, visual assets, offer, CTA and exploitable gaps.

The opportunity is not always "write more text". It may be better proof, architecture, UX, clarity, offer or specialization.

## 9. GEO/AEO

Optimize so answer systems can understand, retrieve and cite useful fragments.

Use precise definitions, direct answers, descriptive headings, self-contained statements, data, examples, sources, comparisons, real FAQs, consistent entities, authorship, dates, visible HTML, representative schema and consistent external brand facts.

Avoid unsupported claims, vague text, paragraphs that depend too heavily on previous context, prompt injection, empty "optimized for AI" claims and accidental blocking of AI search crawlers when citation is the goal.

Measurement: Bing AI Performance, assistant referrals, logs, cited pages, grounding queries, brand mentions, share of voice and assisted conversions.

## 10. Ecommerce SEO

Priorities:

- Indexable categories with demand.
- Products with unique content and correct data.
- Controlled facets.
- Proper variant handling.
- Coherent canonicals.
- Pagination and links to products.
- Product/Offer/Review schema when real.
- Merchant Center/product feeds when relevant.
- Optimized images.
- Visible stock, price and shipping.
- Real reviews.
- Internal linking from hubs, categories and guides.
- Templates that do not create thin content.

Facets: index only combinations with demand, useful content and enough inventory. Use noindex, canonical or blocking depending on the case. Avoid thousands of crawlable URLs without value.

## 11. SaaS SEO

Common clusters:

- Use cases.
- Industries.
- Roles.
- Problems.
- Alternatives.
- Comparisons.
- Integrations.
- Templates.
- Calculators.
- Documentation.
- Security and compliance.

Rules:

- Do not create "alternative to X" pages without honest comparison.
- Do not invent competitor features.
- Connect content with product and conversion.
- Include proof: screenshots, flows, integrations, limits and cases.
- Link TOFU to MOFU to BOFU to signup/demo.

## 12. Local SEO

Review:

- NAP.
- Contact page.
- Google Business Profile.
- Bing Places.
- Reviews.
- Service + location pages.
- Local proof.
- LocalBusiness schema.
- Maps.
- Hours.
- Photos.
- Team.
- Service area.
- Citations/directories.
- Local links.

Do not create city pages without real capacity, local proof or a specific proposition. Avoid doorway pages.

## 13. Publisher and Editorial SEO

Priorities:

- Topical authority.
- Authors.
- Dates.
- Updates.
- Hubs.
- Internal linking.
- Originality.
- Citations.
- Sources.
- Images.
- Video.
- Discover when relevant.
- Cleanup of obsolete content.

Do not update dates without substantial changes. Do not publish summaries of third-party content without original value.

## 14. Marketplaces and Directories

Risks:

- Thin listings.
- Massive duplication.
- Infinite facets.
- Pages without inventory.
- Poor pagination.
- Low-quality UGC.

Actions:

- Minimum thresholds for indexation.
- Noindex for pages without value.
- Internal linking by categories and locations.
- Schema when appropriate.
- Moderation.
- UGC quality.
- Templates with differentiated content.

## 15. SEO Migrations

Pre-launch:

- Inventory, traffic, conversions, keywords, backlinks, 301 mapping, canonicals, metadata, hreflang, schema, sitemaps, robots, Analytics/GSC/BWT, staging and crawl.

Launch:

- Active 301s, no accidental noindex, correct robots, final canonicals, final sitemaps, active tracking, Search Console/Bing submission and smoke test.

Post-launch:

- Live crawl, 404/5xx, logs, GSC/BWT, rankings, conversions, fix queue, baseline comparison and rollback if a critical failure appears.

## 16. Traffic Drops

1. Confirm it is a real organic drop, not tracking loss.
2. Segment by country, device, page type, query, URL and date.
3. Review technical changes.
4. Review Google/Bing updates.
5. Review indexation.
6. Review competition and SERP changes.
7. Review affected content.
8. Review backlinks and authority.
9. Review seasonality.
10. Separate loss of impressions, CTR, ranking and demand.

Do not attribute a drop to a core update without temporal and comparative evidence.

## 17. Reporting

Structure:

- What changed.
- Why it matters.
- Confirmed cause, probable cause and hypotheses.
- What we did.
- What we will do.
- What we need.
- Risks.
- Business metrics.

Avoid reports with metrics that do not support a decision. Every chart should answer a question.
